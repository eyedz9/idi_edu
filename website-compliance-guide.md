## 1. Required Disclosures

### 1.1 Homepage Required Links (Section 74117)

**Regulation**: The institution's homepage must contain "clear and conspicuous" links to items required by Education Code 94913(a).

**Required links on the homepage**:
- School Catalog (PDF)
- School Performance Fact Sheet (PDF)
- Student outcome data / Gainful Employment disclosures
- Student Tuition Recovery Fund (STRF) information
- Any information required by accrediting agencies

**What "clear and conspicuous" means**: A generic footer link labeled "Disclosures" is likely insufficient. The links should be prominently visible to a prospective student visiting the homepage without requiring scrolling through multiple layers of navigation.

**Recommended implementation**: A "Consumer Information" bar or section on the homepage with labeled links to each required document.

---

### 1.2 STRF Verbatim Disclosure (Section 76215)

**Regulation**: Two specific STRF disclosure paragraphs must be provided verbatim. These must appear as actual text, not merely linked as a PDF.

**Required verbatim text (Paragraph 1)**:

> "The State of California established the Student Tuition Recovery Fund (STRF) to relieve or mitigate economic loss suffered by a student in an educational program at a qualifying institution, who is or was a California resident while enrolled, or was enrolled in a residency program, if the student enrolled in the institution, prepaid tuition, and suffered an economic loss. Unless relieved of the obligation to do so, you must pay the state-imposed assessment for the STRF, or it must be paid on your behalf, if you are a student in an educational program, who is a California resident, or are enrolled in a residency program, and prepay all or part of your tuition.
>
> You are not eligible for protection from the STRF and you are not required to pay the STRF assessment, if you are not a California resident, or are not enrolled in a residency program."

**Required verbatim text (Paragraph 2)**:

> "It is important that you keep copies of your enrollment agreement, financial aid documents, receipts, or any other information that documents the amount paid to the institution. Questions regarding the STRF may be directed to the Bureau for Private Postsecondary Education, 1747 N. Market Blvd., Suite 225, Sacramento, CA 95834, (916) 431-6959 or (888) 370-7589.
>
> To be eligible for STRF, you must be a California resident or enrolled in a residency program, prepaid tuition, paid or deemed to have paid the STRF assessment, and suffered an economic loss as a result of any of the following:
> 1. The institution, a location of the institution, or an educational program offered by the institution was closed or discontinued, and you did not choose to participate in a teach-out plan approved by the Bureau or did not complete a chosen teach-out plan approved by the Bureau.
> 2. You were enrolled at an institution or a location of the institution within the 120 day period before the closure of the institution or location of the institution, or were enrolled in an educational program within the 120 day period before the program was discontinued.
> 3. You were enrolled at an institution or a location of the institution more than 120 days before the closure of the institution or location of the institution, in an educational program offered by the institution as to which the Bureau determined there was a significant decline in the quality or value of the program more than 120 days before closure.
> 4. The institution has been ordered to pay a refund by the Bureau but has failed to do so.
> 5. The institution has failed to pay or reimburse loan proceeds under a federal student loan program as required by law, or has failed to pay or reimburse proceeds received by the institution in excess of tuition and other costs.
> 6. You have been awarded restitution, a refund, or other monetary award by an arbitrator or court, based on a violation of this chapter by an institution or representative of an institution, but have been unable to collect the award from the institution.
> 7. You sought legal counsel that resulted in the cancellation of one or more of your student loans and have an invoice for services rendered and evidence of the cancellation of the student loan or loans.
>
> To qualify for STRF reimbursement, the application must be received within four (4) years from the date of the action or event that made the student eligible for recovery from STRF.
>
> A student whose loan is revived by a loan holder or debt collector after a period of noncollection may, at any time, file a written application for recovery from STRF for the debt that would have otherwise been eligible for recovery. If it has been more than four (4) years since the action or event that made the student eligible, the student must have filed a written application for recovery within the original four (4) year period, unless the period has been extended by another act of law.
>
> However, no claim can be paid to any student without a social security number or a taxpayer identification number."

**Where to display**: Disclosures page, and ideally also on the Enrollment/Admissions pages. The text must appear on the page itself, not solely as a linked PDF.

### 1.3 Housing Disclosure (Section 71810(b)(13)(C))

**Regulation**: If an institution has no dormitory facilities, the following must be clearly stated:

> "Interior Designers Institute does not have dormitory facilities under its control and has no responsibility to find or assist a student in finding housing."

**Important**: The regulation explicitly states that merely describing the institution as "non-residential" is **not sufficient**.

**Where to display**: Campus Life page, Admissions page, and/or Disclosures page.

### 1.4 Pre-Enrollment Degree Disclosures (Sections 71775, 71775.5)

For each degree program, the website must disclose:

#### All Programs:
- **Credit Transferability**: "The transferability of credits you earn at Interior Designers Institute is at the complete discretion of an institution to which you may seek to transfer. Acceptance of the degree, diploma, or certificate you earn is also at the complete discretion of the institution to which you may seek to transfer. If the credits or degree, diploma, or certificate that you earn at this institution are not accepted at the institution to which you seek to transfer, you may be required to repeat some or all of your coursework at that institution. For this reason you should make certain that your attendance at this institution will meet your educational goals. This may include contacting an institution to which you may seek to transfer after attending Interior Designers Institute to determine if your credits or degree, diploma, or certificate will transfer."

#### Certificate Course:
- This program is classified as avocational (personal enrichment) and is not designed to lead to employment
- This is not a degree program; completion does not confer a degree
- The Certificate does not guarantee admission to IDI's degree programs

#### Associate of Arts:
- Credit transferability warning (above)
- Whether the AA meets requirements for any professional licensure in California

#### Bachelor of Arts:
- Credit transferability warning (above)
- CIDA accreditation status and its relationship to professional practice
- Whether the BA meets requirements for professional licensure in California
- Note about General Education requirement (45 quarter units from an accredited institution)

#### Master of Interior Architecture:
- Credit transferability warning (above)
- Terminal degree statement
- Whether the MIA meets requirements for professional licensure in California

**Where to display**: Each program detail page (`/programs/[slug]`), Admissions page.

### 1.5 Performance Fact Sheet (Section 74112)

**Requirements**:
- Must be provided to prospective students prior to enrollment
- Must be prominently available on the website
- Format: 12pt type minimum, 1.15 line spacing minimum, bold 14pt headings
- Must include completion rates, placement rates, licensure/certification rates
- Employment data must reference Standard Occupational Classification (SOC) codes

**Where to link**: Homepage (per Section 74117), each program detail page, Admissions page, Disclosures page.

---

### 1.6 School Catalog Availability (Section 71810)

**Required catalog content** (15+ items including):
1. Name, address, phone, website of institution
2. Date of publication / effective dates
3. Approval/accreditation status with agency names
4. Program descriptions including objectives, hours, units
5. Faculty qualifications
6. Admission requirements for each program
7. Cancellation, withdrawal, and refund policies
8. Grading standards and satisfactory progress requirements
9. Tuition and fees with payment terms
10. Student services available
11. Placement assistance availability (and limitations)
12. Library and learning resources
13. Housing statement (see Section 1.3 above)
14. Student grievance procedure
15. Facilities and equipment description

**Where to display**: Catalog PDF must be linked from Homepage, Admissions page, and Disclosures page. A notice like "View our complete School Catalog" should be visible on admissions-related pages.

### 1.7 Cancellation and Refund Policy (Section 71810(b)(11))

**Regulation**: The institution's refund policy, including buyer's right to cancel and refund schedule, must be disclosed.

**Key elements**:
- Right to cancel within specific timeframes
- Refund calculation methodology
- Pro-rata refund for students who withdraw
- Timeline for refund disbursement

**Where to display**: Tuition & Fees page and/or Disclosures page.

### 1.10 Self-Employment Disclosure (Section 74112(c))

**Regulation**: If graduates are commonly self-employed, the Performance Fact Sheet must include a verbatim disclosure:

> "This program may result in freelance or self-employment."
> "[Description of the type(s) of self-employment for which the program prepares its graduates.]"
> "The work available to graduates of this program is frequently freelance or self-employed work. This type of work may not be consistent. The period of employment can range from one day to weeks to several months. Hours worked in a day or week may be more or fewer than the traditional 8-hour work day or 40-hour work week. You can expect to spend unpaid time expanding your networks, marketing yourself, and applying for new projects. Once graduates begin to develop a network of clients, freelance work may become more consistent. Some graduates of this program choose to start their own interior design businesses."

**Where to display**: Performance Fact Sheet (PDF format requirement applies), and consider including on relevant program pages.

## 2. Prohibited Claims & Representations

### 2.1 Employment Guarantees (Section 74140, 94897)

**PROHIBITED**:
- Guaranteeing or implying that graduates will obtain employment
- Promising specific salary levels
- Claiming 100% placement rates
- Implying that enrollment guarantees a specific career outcome
- Using language like "you WILL design hotels" instead of "you MAY pursue..."

**Current concern**: Program career outcomes in `src/data/programs.ts` are written as if describing what graduates will definitely do (e.g., "Design hotels and resorts that guests never want to leave", "Lead design teams as a senior designer"). These should be reframed as potential career paths.

**Compliant alternatives**:
- "Career paths may include..."
- "Graduates may pursue roles such as..."
- "This program prepares students for careers in..."
- Add disclaimer: "Career outcomes are not guaranteed and depend on individual qualifications, market conditions, and other factors."

---

### 2.2 Misleading Statistics (Section 74140)

**PROHIBITED**:
- Publishing statistics that cannot be verified
- Using outdated placement or completion data
- Cherry-picking favorable metrics

**Current concern**: The "1000+ Graduates & Counting" statistic in `src/data/why-idi.ts` must be verifiable. The description "In firms, studios, and businesses they built themselves" implies employment outcomes without data.

**Fix**: Ensure all statistics are sourced from verifiable institutional records. Add a footnote or asterisk with the data source and date.

---

### 2.3 Misrepresenting Accreditation (Section 74140)

**PROHIBITED**:
- Implying a degree is equivalent to one from a different type of institution
- Misrepresenting the scope of accreditation
- Falsely claiming accreditation from bodies that have not granted it

**Current status**: IDI's accreditation claims are factually accurate. ACCSC accredits all programs; CIDA accredits the BA only. This distinction is properly maintained.

---

### 2.4 Use of Term "University" (Section 74150)

**PROHIBITED**: Using the term "university" in the institution's name or marketing unless specifically authorized.

**Current status**: COMPLIANT. IDI uses "Institute" and does not claim university status.

---

### 2.5 Avocational Program Misrepresentation

**PROHIBITED**: Representing an avocational program as leading to employment or a professional credential.

**Current concern**: The Certificate Course is flagged as `isAvocational: true` but the `careerNote` states: "Many certificate graduates have launched their own design businesses or landed roles in the industry." This contradicts the avocational classification.

**Fix**: Replace with language that accurately reflects the personal enrichment purpose: "This program is designed for personal enrichment and exploration of interior design. It is not designed to lead to employment. Students interested in pursuing design as a career should consider our degree programs."

## 3. Advertising Rules

### 3.1 Definition of Advertising (Section 74140)

**All website content is classified as "advertising"** under California regulations. This includes:
- Page text and marketing copy
- Images and videos
- Testimonials
- Statistics and claims
- Social media posts linked from the website
- Email campaigns linked from the website

### 3.2 Retention Requirement

All advertising materials (including web content) must be **retained for 5 years** from the date of last use.

**Recommendation**: Implement a web archiving strategy:
- Regular Wayback Machine submissions
- Internal content snapshots with timestamps
- Git commit history as a secondary record
- Add "Last Updated" dates to key disclosure pages

### 3.3 Required Elements in All Advertising

All advertising must include:
- The institution's official name as approved by BPPE
- The institution's physical address

**Current status**: COMPLIANT. The Footer includes "Interior Designers Institute" and the full address.

### 3.4 Prohibited Advertising Practices

The following are prohibited in all advertising (including website content):

| Prohibited | Example | Status |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| False or misleading statements | Guaranteeing employment | REVIEW NEEDED |
| Unsubstantiated superlatives | "California's Premier" | REVIEW NEEDED |
| Implying government endorsement | "Government approved" | COMPLIANT |
| Bait-and-switch tactics | Advertising unavailable programs | COMPLIANT |
| Misleading use of "free" | Claiming free programs with hidden costs | COMPLIANT |
| Testimonials without substantiation | Using fake or unverified testimonials | VERIFY NEEDED |

### 3.5 Superlative Claims

**Regulation**: Claims like "best," "premier," "number one," or "top" must be substantiated with verifiable evidence.

**Current concerns**:
- `SITE_TAGLINE`: "California's Premier Interior Design School" -- "Premier" is a superlative
- "The gold standard in design education" (multiple locations)
- "The highest seal of approval in design education"

**Recommendation**: Replace with factual, verifiable statements:
- "California's Dedicated Interior Design School Since 1984"
- "CIDA is the recognized accrediting body for interior design education in North America"
- "CIDA accreditation is widely recognized by employers and licensing bodies"

## 4. Use of Accreditation Status

### 4.1 Accurate Representation

**Requirements**:
- Must distinguish between institutional accreditation (ACCSC -- all programs) and program-specific accreditation (CIDA -- BA only)
- Must not imply CIDA accreditation applies to programs other than the BA
- Must include the full name of accrediting bodies when first referenced
- Must include URLs for accrediting bodies

**Current status**: COMPLIANT. The website correctly distinguishes between ACCSC (institutional) and CIDA (BA only).

### 4.2 ACCSC Standards

Per ACCSC Standards of Accreditation:
- The institution must accurately state its accredited status
- Must not misrepresent the meaning or scope of accreditation
- Must include ACCSC name and contact information in relevant disclosures
- Must disclose any pending accreditation actions or probationary status

### 4.3 BPPE Approval

**Requirements**:
- Must state approval to operate by BPPE
- Must include BPPE School Code: 2302631
- Must not state or imply that BPPE approval constitutes an endorsement of the institution

**Current status**: COMPLIANT. School code is displayed on the Contact page and Accreditation page.

## 5. Student Outcome Reporting

### 5.1 Performance Fact Sheet Requirements (Section 74112)

Each Performance Fact Sheet must include:
- **Completion rates** for each program
- **Placement rates** (employment in the field of training)
- **Licensure/certification examination pass rates** (if applicable)
- **SOC codes** for reported job categories
- **Median earnings** of graduates (where available)
- **Salary and wage information** (if reported)

### 5.2 Format Requirements

- Minimum 12pt type
- Minimum 1.15 line spacing
- Section headings in bold, minimum 14pt type
- Must be available as a document (PDF) provided before enrollment

### 5.3 Gainful Employment Disclosures

For each Title IV eligible program, the website should provide:
- Program name and credential earned
- Total cost of attendance
- On-time completion rate
- Job placement rate
- Median loan debt of program completers
- Median earnings of program completers

**Current status**: Gainful Employment disclosure PDFs are listed in `src/data/disclosures.ts` for each program. These should be directly linked from each program's detail page.

### 5.4 Career Outcome Claims

**Rules**:
- Any claim about employment, salary, or career outcomes must be supported by verifiable data
- Claims must match the data in the Performance Fact Sheet
- If graduates commonly work in self-employment, the self-employment disclosure (Section 1.10) must be included

## Appendix B: SB 1192 Considerations

SB 1192 (effective January 1, 2025) introduced additional requirements for private postsecondary institutions:
- Enhanced disclosure requirements for student outcomes
- Stricter advertising standards
- Additional consumer protection measures

All SB 1192 requirements have been incorporated into the relevant sections of this guide.

## Appendix C: SB 607 (Ability to Benefit)

SB 607 modified Ability to Benefit (ATB) provisions. Key website implications:
- If IDI accepts ATB students, the ATB pathway and eligibility criteria must be disclosed
- ATB students must receive additional disclosures about financial aid eligibility
- The institution must clearly state which programs accept ATB students (if any)

**Current status**: The IDI website does not reference ATB pathways. If IDI does not accept ATB students, no changes are needed. If IDI does accept ATB students, disclosures must be added to the Admissions and Financial Aid pages.
