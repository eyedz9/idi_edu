### Narrative

- **Overline:** Our Story
- **Title:** The IDI Story

It started with a conviction: that interior design deserved a school as intentional as the spaces it teaches you to create. In 1984, Judy Deaton founded Interior Designers Institute in Corona del Mar, California — a small, fiercely focused school where every student was known, every critique was personal, and the line between classroom and studio didn't exist.

By 1990, the Institute had outgrown its original home and moved to Newport Beach — right where it belongs. Nestled at 1061 Camelback Street, the campus sits in one of Southern California's richest design ecosystems: showrooms, design centers, and a community of architects and designers who still serve as guest critics, mentors, and employers for IDI students.

What began as a single certificate program has grown into four tiers of design education — Certificate, Associate of Arts, Bachelor of Arts, and Master of Interior Architecture — without ever losing the intimacy that made it work in the first place. Class sizes stayed small on purpose. Faculty stayed practicing professionals by design.

The accolades followed: institutional accreditation from ACCSC, CIDA accreditation for the BA program, Title IV federal financial aid approval. Not because IDI chased credentials, but because the education earned them.

Four decades in, thousands of graduates are shaping interiors across Southern California and beyond — in homes, hotels, hospitals, offices, and studios of their own. The mission hasn't changed. The talent pool keeps getting deeper.

### Key Dates Highlight

1. **1984** — Founded in Corona del Mar, California, by Judy Deaton with a mission to provide quality interior design education.
2. **1990** — Relocated to 1061 Camelback Street in Newport Beach, placing students at the heart of Southern California's design community.
3. **2026** — Over four decades of excellence in interior design education with thousands of successful graduates shaping spaces worldwide.

### Courses (22)

1. **125 — Designing Phase I** (4 units): Your design vocabulary starts here. Dive into the principles and elements that make a room work — color, proportion, rhythm, balance. Learn to read a space the way a musician reads a score, then start composing your own.
2. **126 — Designing Phase II** (4 units): Push deeper. Tackle more ambitious residential concepts, source materials with confidence, and develop the kind of design process that turns a blank floor plan into a living, breathing space.
3. **200 — Architectural Drafting** (4 units): Floor plans, elevations, sections, details — the technical drawings that turn ideas into buildable reality. Master the manual drafting conventions that every design professional needs in their toolkit.
4. **201 — History of Interiors and Architecture I** (4 units): From Egyptian temples to Georgian townhouses, trace how civilizations shaped the spaces they lived in. Understand the cultural DNA embedded in every column, arch, and textile pattern from antiquity through the 18th century.
5. **202 — History of Interiors and Architecture II** (4 units): Bauhaus. Art Deco. Mid-century modern. Minimalism. Follow the revolutions that brought us from the 19th century to today — and discover how the past keeps showing up in the most forward-looking designs.
6. **203 — Residential Design** (4 units): Homes are personal. They're emotional. And designing one well requires mastering space planning, furniture layout, kitchen and bath logic, and material choices that serve both beauty and daily life.
7. **205 — Commercial Design I** (4 units): Step beyond the home. Design offices that fuel productivity, retail spaces that move product, and hospitality environments that make guests linger. Learn the codes, ADA requirements, and specs that commercial work demands.
8. **206 — Commercial Design II** (4 units): Scale up. Take on larger commercial environments where programming, project management, and professional documentation separate the good designers from the great ones.
9. **207 — Materials Specification** (4 units): Marble or porcelain? Hardwood or engineered? Every surface tells a story. Learn to select, specify, and defend your material choices — balancing aesthetics, performance, sustainability, and budget.
10. **208 — Construction Principles** (4 units): Understand what's behind the walls. Structural systems, HVAC, plumbing, electrical — the invisible architecture that shapes every design decision you'll ever make.
11. **209 — Design Thinking** (4 units): Great design solves problems you didn't know you had. Develop the creative frameworks, critical thinking muscle, and evidence-based strategies that separate decorating from designing.
12. **210 — Color** (4 units): Color changes everything — mood, proportion, temperature, emotion. Go deep into color theory, color systems, and the psychology of hue. Then put it to work in spaces that feel exactly right.
13. **212 — Perspective** (4 units): Learn to draw what the eye actually sees. One-point, two-point, three-point — master the perspective techniques that make your design presentations leap off the page.
14. **215 — Spaceplanning** (4 units): How people move through a room matters as much as how it looks. Study programming, adjacency, circulation, and furniture arrangement — the invisible choreography of well-designed space.
15. **216 — SketchUp** (4 units): Build your designs in three dimensions before a single nail gets hammered. Learn SketchUp's modeling tools to visualize, iterate, and present interior concepts that clients can walk through virtually.
16. **217 — Business Principles** (4 units): Talent gets you noticed. Business sense gets you paid. Cover the essentials — marketing, client management, contracts, ethics, and the project management skills that keep your practice thriving.
17. **218 — Textiles** (4 units): Fabric is where design gets tactile. Study fibers, weaves, finishes, and dyes. Learn to specify textiles that satisfy the hand, the eye, the code inspector, and the client — all at once.
18. **220 — Healthcare Design** (4 units): Design that heals. Explore the specialized demands of hospitals, clinics, and wellness centers — where evidence-based design, infection control, and human comfort converge.
19. **221 — Photoshop** (4 units): Turn raw images into polished presentations. Master Photoshop for design rendering, mood boards, and the visual storytelling that wins client buy-in.
20. **222 — Building Codes** (4 units): Codes aren't constraints — they're the rules of the game. Learn building codes, fire codes, ADA standards, and life safety requirements so your designs are as safe as they are stunning.
21. **223 — Environmental Design** (4 units): Design with the planet in mind. Explore LEED principles, sustainable materials, energy efficiency, and indoor air quality — because beautiful spaces should never come at the earth's expense.
22. **225 — Computer-Aided Drafting I** (6 units): AutoCAD is the industry's lingua franca. Learn to produce precise floor plans, elevations, sections, and details using the software that every design firm expects you to know.
