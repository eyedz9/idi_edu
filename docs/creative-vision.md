# IDI Creative Vision: "The Gallery Experience"

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** February 11, 2026
**Creative Director:** IDI Digital Team

## 1. Creative Concept: The Gallery Experience

The Interior Designers Institute website should feel like walking through a curated design exhibition. Each section is a **"room"** with its own mood, lighting, and content. The transition between rooms (scroll) should feel intentional and dramatic.

### Core Metaphor

A visitor doesn't browse our site --- they *move through it*. The homepage is a gallery opening night: dramatic lighting, provocative work on the walls, the buzz of creative energy in the air. Program pages are private studios where you see the craft up close. The admissions funnel is the VIP room --- exclusive, warm, action-oriented.

### Guiding Principles

- **Every pixel is portfolio.** The site itself is proof of IDI's design philosophy. If a section doesn't demonstrate design excellence, it doesn't belong.
- **Scroll is choreography.** Content reveals should feel like curtains drawing back, not loading screens filling in.
- **The site teaches.** A prospective student should absorb design principles just by navigating --- asymmetry, hierarchy, negative space, material contrast.

### Room Breakdown

| Section | Room Metaphor | Mood | Dominant Color |
|---------|---------------|------|----------------|
| Hero / Homepage | Gallery entrance | Dramatic, inviting | Plum + Amber |
| Programs | Studio tour | Focused, aspirational | Plum + Violet |
| Campus Life | Living room | Warm, social | Warm neutrals + Jade |
| Admissions | VIP lounge | Exclusive, action-oriented | Amber-forward |
| About / Faculty | Private collection | Authoritative, personal | Plum + Parchment |
| Contact | Concierge desk | Helpful, direct | Clean, bright |

## 2. Brand Personality

IDI's voice is **Confident, Creative, Warm, and Bold.**

Not stuffy academic. Not Silicon Valley startup. Think: **the cool professor who actually practices what they teach.**

### Voice Characteristics

| Trait | What It Sounds Like | What It Doesn't Sound Like |
|-------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| **Confident** | "We've shaped 4,500+ careers in interior design." | "We believe we may be a good option for your education." |
| **Creative** | "Design isn't decoration. It's how people feel in a room." | "Our curriculum covers a comprehensive range of topics." |
| **Warm** | "Welcome to the place where your portfolio starts." | "Submit your application to begin the enrollment process." |
| **Bold** | "Newport Beach. Where your classroom has an ocean view." | "Conveniently located in Southern California." |

### Tone Spectrum

- **Headlines:** Bold, provocative, sometimes poetic. Use fragments. Break conventions.
- **Body copy:** Clear, warm, conversational. Never more than 3 sentences without a visual break.
- **CTAs:** Direct with a creative edge. "Start Your Portfolio" > "Apply Now." "See the Studios" > "Learn More."
- **Microcopy:** Playful, human. Error messages should charm, not frustrate.

## 3. Visual Principles

These six principles govern every design decision on the site.

### 3.1 Asymmetry Over Symmetry

Centered layouts feel corporate. Offset grids, broken columns, and intentional imbalance feel *designed*. Use bento grids, overlapping elements, and variable column widths. Symmetry is permitted only when it's deliberate and dramatic (e.g., a full-bleed hero image).

### 3.2 Texture Over Flatness

Every dark surface should have depth --- grain overlays, mesh gradients, subtle noise. Flat color blocks are banned on dark backgrounds. Light sections use paper-like texture, linen feels, or subtle warm noise. The site should feel tactile, like you could reach out and touch the materials.

### 3.3 Warmth Over Clinical

Cool grays and pure whites are replaced with ivory, cream, parchment, and sandstone. Even shadows should lean warm (plum-tinted, not gray). Photography is warm-toned. The overall impression should be a well-lit design showroom, not a hospital corridor.

### 3.4 Drama Over Safety

Safe means forgettable. Use scale contrast (massive type against small details), bold color blocking, and provocative imagery. Dark sections aren't just "dark mode" --- they're mood lighting. Hero sections should make you stop scrolling. If a layout feels "fine," it's not done.

### 3.5 Space Over Clutter

Negative space is a design tool, not wasted real estate. Let elements breathe. A single powerful image with generous whitespace outperforms a grid of six mediocre ones. Section padding should be generous (120-200px vertical). Information density decreases as emotional impact increases.

### 3.6 Motion Over Static

Movement conveys life, energy, and sophistication. Static sites feel like brochures; animated sites feel like experiences. But motion must always serve content --- never distract from it. Every animation needs a reason to exist.

## 4. Color Strategy: Plum Luxe Palette

### Primary Palette

| Token | Hex | Name | Role |
|-------|-----|------|------|
| `plum-900` | `#120B1E` | Plum Noir | Deepest background, hero sections, footer |
| `plum-800` | `#1E1230` | Midnight Plum | Cards, secondary dark surfaces |
| `plum-700` | `#2A1B40` | Velvet | Elevated surfaces, dark mode cards |
| `amber-500` | `#F5A623` | Ember Amber | Primary CTA, action, energy |
| `amber-400` | `#FFCC66` | Honey | Hover states, highlights |
| `jade` | `#3DD6A3` | Jade | Success, growth, accreditation |
| `violet` | `#B06CFF` | Electric Violet | Creative accent, events, programs |
| `parchment` | `#EDE8E0` | Parchment | Primary body text on dark |
| `sandstone` | `#A89F94` | Sandstone | Muted/secondary text |

### When to Use Each Color

#### Amber --- Action & Energy
- All primary CTAs ("Apply Now," "Request Info," "Start Your Journey")
- Featured content highlights and badges
- Section dividers and accent rules
- Icon accents for important features
- Navigation hover states
- Price/value callouts

#### Jade --- Success & Growth
- Accreditation badges and trust signals
- "New" tags and fresh content indicators
- Success messages and confirmations
- Statistics and achievement numbers
- Scholarship and financial aid sections
- Form validation success states

#### Violet --- Creativity & Distinction
- Featured program cards and program page accents
- Event announcements and special content
- Student spotlight and creative work
- Special offers or limited-time content
- Gallery/portfolio elements
- Creative section backgrounds (as gradient wash)

#### Plum Darks --- Sophistication & Depth
- Hero backgrounds (with mesh gradient overlay)
- Header and footer
- Premium content sections
- Full-bleed dramatic moments
- Photo overlay backgrounds
- Modal/dialog backgrounds

#### Ivory & Cream (Warm-50, Warm-100) --- Breathing Room
- Main content area backgrounds
- Card backgrounds on dark sections (inverted)
- Form backgrounds
- Reading-heavy sections
- Between dramatic dark sections (palette cleanser)
- Mobile-first readable sections

### Color Ratios by Section Type

- **Hero sections:** 70% Plum, 20% Amber, 10% Parchment
- **Content sections:** 70% Ivory, 20% Parchment text, 10% Accent
- **CTA sections:** 60% Plum, 30% Amber, 10% White
- **Footer:** 80% Plum, 10% Amber, 10% Sandstone

## 5. Typography Strategy

### Type Pairing

| Font | Role | Character |
|------|------|-----------|
| **Playfair Display** | Headings, display text, editorial moments | Editorial authority, luxury, heritage. The font says "we've been here, and we're excellent." |
| **Inter** | Body text, UI, navigation, buttons | Clarity, modernity, readability. The font says "we respect your time and attention." |

### Scale Contrast Philosophy

The tension between massive headings and refined body text creates visual drama and hierarchy. Headlines should feel like magazine covers; body text should feel like a well-set book.

### Heading Scale

| Level | Size Range | Weight | Use Case |
|-------|-----------|--------|----------|
| Display / Hero | `8-15vw` (clamp: 3rem-12rem) | Bold (700) | Homepage hero, section openers |
| H1 | `clamp(2.5rem, 5vw, 4.5rem)` | Bold (700) | Page titles |
| H2 | `clamp(2rem, 4vw, 3.5rem)` | Bold (700) | Section headings |
| H3 | `clamp(1.5rem, 3vw, 2rem)` | Semibold (600) | Subsection headings |
| H4 | `1.25rem` | Semibold (600) | Card titles, feature headings |

### Body Scale

| Element | Size | Line Height | Letter Spacing |
|---------|------|------------|----------------|
| Body Large | `1.125rem` (18px) | `1.75` | `0` |
| Body Default | `1rem` (16px) | `1.6` | `0` |
| Body Small | `0.875rem` (14px) | `1.5` | `0.01em` |
| Caption | `0.75rem` (12px) | `1.4` | `0.02em` |
| Overline | `0.75rem` (12px) | `1.4` | `0.15em` uppercase |

### Typography Rules

1. **Never use more than 2 font weights on a single component.** Typically: Bold heading + Regular body.
2. **Headings on dark backgrounds use `text-parchment`.** Never pure white.
3. **Overlines above headings use `text-amber-500` with `uppercase tracking-widest`.** This signals section starts.
4. **Pull quotes use Playfair Display Italic at 2-3x body size** with amber left-border accent.
5. **Maximum line length: 65 characters** for body text (use `max-w-prose` or `max-w-2xl`).

## 6. Animation Philosophy

### Core Belief

Every animation must have purpose --- never decorative. Animation is communication. It tells the user: "this is important," "this is connected to that," "you've arrived somewhere new."

### Animation Categories

#### Entrance Animations --- "Curtains Opening"
Content should reveal like a gallery unveiling. Preferred entrance patterns:
- **Clip-path reveals:** Content slides in from behind a mask (bottom-to-top, left-to-right)
- **Staggered fade-up:** Elements appear sequentially with 100-150ms delays
- **Scale from 0.95:** Subtle zoom-in with fade creates presence
- **Never:** Bounce, flip, spin, or any animation that draws attention to itself

#### Scroll Animations --- "Turning Pages"
Scrolling between sections should feel like moving through rooms:
- **Parallax depth:** Background elements move slower than foreground (subtle, max 20% offset)
- **Section color transitions:** Background color shifts smoothly as user scrolls between light and dark sections
- **Progressive reveal:** Long content reveals line by line or paragraph by paragraph
- **Never:** Hijack scroll, snap-scroll entire viewports, or create scroll jank

#### Micro-interactions --- "Tactile Feedback"
Small interactions should feel physical:
- **Magnetic buttons:** CTA buttons subtly pull toward the cursor on hover
- **Hover reveals:** Cards show additional information layers on hover
- **Focus rings:** Visible, animated, amber-colored focus indicators
- **Form inputs:** Labels float, borders glow amber on focus
- **Never:** Delay interaction feedback, animate something the user didn't trigger

#### Page Transitions --- "Walking Between Rooms"
Navigation between pages should feel continuous:
- **Fade through plum:** Brief dark flash between pages (like walking through a doorway)
- **Content cascade:** New page content staggers in from top to bottom
- **Shared element transitions:** If clicking a card, the card image/title should morph into the page header

### Timing & Easing

| Animation Type | Duration | Easing |
|---------------|----------|--------|
| Micro-interaction (hover, focus) | 150-200ms | `ease-out` |
| Entrance (fade, slide) | 400-600ms | `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` |
| Section transition | 600-800ms | `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` |
| Page transition | 300-500ms | `ease-in-out` |

### Accessibility: prefers-reduced-motion

**This is non-negotiable.** Every animated element must respect `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`:
- Disable all transform/translate animations
- Reduce opacity transitions to instant or near-instant (<100ms)
- Keep essential state changes (open/close) but remove flourishes
- GSAP timelines should check `matchMedia('(prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)')` and use alternative timelines

```css
@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  *, *::before, *::after {
    animation-duration: 0.01ms !important;
    animation-iteration-count: 1 !important;
    transition-duration: 0.01ms !important;
    scroll-behavior: auto !important;
  }
}
```

## 7. Photography Direction

### Interior Spaces --- Aspirational but Achievable

- Spaces should look like they were designed by an IDI graduate --- beautiful, functional, lived-in
- Avoid sterile, magazine-perfect shots. Show texture, warmth, personality
- Lighting should be warm and natural, never fluorescent
- Include design details: material transitions, hardware, textile close-ups
- Spaces should represent diverse styles: modern, transitional, coastal, eclectic

### Student Photography --- Diversity & Creative Energy

- Students should look engaged, not posed. Candid > portrait
- Show the creative process: sketching, model-building, material selection, critiques
- Represent the full diversity of IDI's student body: age, ethnicity, gender, background
- Capture collaboration and community, not isolated individuals
- Show students in aspirational settings: studios, Newport Beach, design showrooms

### Newport Beach --- Paradise for Designers

- Showcase the unique advantage of IDI's location
- Coastal light, architecture, landscapes, lifestyle
- Connect environment to design inspiration: "Your classroom has an ocean view"
- Golden hour photography preferred
- Include local design landmarks, shops, galleries

### Materials & Textures --- Close-up, Tactile, Sensory

- Fabric swatches, wood grains, stone surfaces, metal hardware
- Shot close enough to feel the texture through the screen
- Use as background elements, section dividers, or hero overlays
- Color-graded to match the Plum Luxe palette (warm, rich tones)
- These images bridge the gap between digital and physical design

### Photography Processing Guidelines

- **Color temperature:** Warm (5500-6500K)
- **Contrast:** Medium-high, never flat
- **Saturation:** Slightly boosted in warm tones, desaturated in cool tones
- **Shadows:** Lifted slightly, plum-tinted on dark images
- **Grain:** Subtle film grain overlay on hero images for texture
- **Crop:** Tight and intentional. Every crop is a design decision.
