## About IDI

### History

Interior Designers Institute was founded in **1984** in Corona del Mar,
California. The school relocated to its current **Newport Beach**
location in **1990**, establishing itself as a boutique college
dedicated exclusively to interior design education.

Over four decades, IDI has maintained its commitment to small class
sizes, personalized instruction, and hands-on learning from working
design professionals. The school has developed active student chapters
of **ASID** (American Society of Interior Designers) and **IIDA**
(International Interior Design Association), providing students with
professional networking opportunities before graduation.

IDI remains **Orange County\'s only dedicated interior design school**
and one of only four CIDA-accredited programs in Southern California.

serving as the prerequisite for the Bachelor\'s degree. The curriculum

### Master of Interior Architecture

**Duration:** 12-15 months\
**Total Credits:** 45 quarter units\
**Total Cost:** \$22,600\
**GPA Requirement:** Cumulative 3.0 (minimum 2.0 per course)\
**Format:** Part-time, in-class only (Monday/Thursday evenings)

The Master of Interior Architecture (MIA) is IDI\'s highest degree,
designed for working professionals seeking advanced credentials. This
intensive program advances research and analytical skills, promotes
independent and critical thinking, and prepares graduates to make
meaningful contributions to the interior design field.

#### Admission Requirements

- Bachelor\'s degree in interior design, interior architecture, or
  architecture from a US college/university

- Portfolio review required

- Personal interview required

- Proficiency in AutoCAD, Photoshop, and 3D CAD software

#### Program Features

- Small class size with limited enrollment

- Hybrid/Evening classes accommodate working professionals

- Advanced curriculum beyond BA-level academics

- Culminates in capstone design project

- Student exhibit and verbal presentation required

#### Degree Requirements

- Must be granted Candidacy by Graduate Committee

- Fulfill all financial obligations

- Complete capstone project with exhibit and presentation

### Transfer Students

IDI evaluates transfer credit on an individual basis. Two types of
credit may be transferred:

1.  **Design-related credit** from prior interior design coursework

2.  **General education credit** for BA program requirements

#### Evaluation Process

- ~~Free~~ Credit evaluation available upon completion of Transfer
  Application

- Official transcripts required prior to entering degree program

- Design credit based on **portfolio review + prior college credit**
  (never solely on transcript)

- Only courses with grade of **\"C\" or better** accepted

- Final acceptance at Institute discretion
